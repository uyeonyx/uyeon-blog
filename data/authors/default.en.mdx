<IntroCard>

An engineer who seeks to understand and design the world through autonomy, structure, and deep focus.

My curiosity always begins with the question: **"Why does it work this way?"** Whether writing code, designing organizations, or optimizing productivity—I see it all as systems. I'm not someone who stops at adding features. I find greater joy in reimagining the **fundamental structures and principles** that enable things to work.

I shine brightest when I enter **hyperfocus**. The process of staring at tangled complexity until I find a simple, elegant solution that captures only what's essential—that's both my creative method and my pleasure. I don't generate many ideas; instead, I think by connecting patterns across different domains and rebuilding frameworks from the ground up.

Even when I'm not working, I'm still designing the **meta-structure** of work itself. I don't stop engineering—my attention simply shifts toward re-architecting how engineering gets done.

This blog is a trace of that exploration. A record of seeing the world as structure, reconstructing it through principles, and imagining better systems.

</IntroCard>
